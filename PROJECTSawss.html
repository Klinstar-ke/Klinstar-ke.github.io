MSES Business Dashboard

A data story of Micro and Small Enterprises in Kenya

Introduction

This project explores micro and small enterprises (MSEs) in Kenya using a dataset with attributes such as business location, education, gender, family status, business size, employment, and economic activity. The goal is to uncover patterns and build dashboards in Power BI that communicate insights for policy-makers, investors, and entrepreneurs.

Data & Methodology

Tools Used: Power BI, Tableau, Excel, SQL.
Dataset: Survey data from Micro and Small Enterprises Sector (MSES).
Approach: Data cleaning, KPI creation, and dashboard storytelling with drill-down capability.

Key KPIs & Insights

Total Businesses: 2,000 registered enterprises
Average Size of Business: 8 employees
% Urban vs % Rural: Urban 49% | Rural 51%
Average Age of Owners: 34 years
Top Economic Activities: Agriculture, Trade, and Services

Conclusion & Next Steps

This analysis highlights the importance of small enterprises in rural areas, the youth-driven entrepreneurship trend, and the dominant role of agriculture and trade. Future analysis can integrate time-series forecasting to predict growth, and clustering models to identify enterprise success patterns.
